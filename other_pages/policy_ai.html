World's First Comprehensive AI Regulation

Prohibited AI

Unacceptable risk applications (e.g., social scoring)

High-Risk AI

Medical devices fall here - strict requirements

General-Purpose AI

Foundation models with transparency requirements

Low/No Risk

Minimal requirements (e.g., spam filters)

Extraterritorial Reach

Applies to providers or users outside the EU if AI outputs are used within the EU

2.4 WHO Guidance on AI for Health

Ethics and Governance of LMMs (2024)

Guidance for large multi-modal models (LMMs) with 40+ recommendations

For Governments

  • Invest in public data infrastructure with ethical access
  • Use laws to ensure LMMs meet ethical standards
  • Assign regulatory agencies for approval
  • Mandate post-release auditing and impact assessments

For Developers

  • Engage diverse stakeholders
  • Define tasks narrowly
  • Ensure accuracy and anticipate secondary outcomes

WHO Considerations for AI Regulation (2023)

Transparency and documentation across product lifecycle

Comprehensive risk management addressing intended use, continuous learning and cybersecurity

External validation of data

Emphasizes collaboration among regulators, patients, healthcare professionals and industry

Global Initiative on AI for Health (GI-AI4H)

Launched in 2023 to harmonize governance standards across UN member states

Ethics

Guard against risks, promote human rights, create training programs

Regulation

Harmonize fragmented global policies

Implementation

Culturally adapted deployments, especially in LMICs

Operations

Support member states with local capacity building

Six Foundational Principles (2021)

Protect Autonomy

Promote Well-being

Ensure Transparency

Foster Responsibility

Ensure Inclusiveness

Promote Sustainability

Forms the ethical foundation for all WHO AI guidance

3 Implementation and Evaluation Frameworks

Key Frameworks for Clinical AI

SALIENT Framework

Integrated framework with stages and cross-stage elements including governance and policy

Derived from meta-analysis of 83 clinical AI reports

DECIDE-AI

17 AI-specific and 10 generic items for early-stage clinical evaluation

Bridges the "AI chasm"

SPIRIT-AI

Adds 15 items to trial protocol guidelines for AI interventions

Complements CONSORT-AI

TEHAI

Translational evaluation focusing on capability, utility and adoption

Emphasizes safety and context

FAIR-AI

Practical evaluation framework for health systems (2025)

Living document

Clinical Trials Framework

4-phase framework derived from clinical trials methodology

Safety → Efficacy → Effectiveness → Monitoring

SALIENT Framework Components

Stages

Development Procurement Testing Deployment

Cross-Stage Elements

Governance Policy Regulation Ethics Auditability

Provides actionable checklists for each stage and emphasizes policy coordination across the AI lifecycle

Clinical Trials-Informed Framework

1

Phase 1 – Safety

Retrospective or silent-mode evaluation to assess safety and fairness

2

Phase 2 – Efficacy

Prospective studies in live clinical environments (background operation)

3

Phase 3 – Effectiveness

Broader deployment comparing AI with standard care

4

Phase 4 – Monitoring

Continuous post-deployment monitoring for model drift and bias

4 Real-World Deployments and Clinical Evidence

Penda Health & GPT-4 Beta Study (Kenya)

AI Consult Tool

Powered by OpenAI's GPT-4o, runs in background during consultations

Results

Reduced diagnostic errors by 16% and treatment errors by 13% in ~40,000 patients

Local Adaptation

Incorporated local disease prevalence and guidelines to build clinician trust

Approved by Kenyan regulators - one of the earliest large-scale evaluations of generative AI in real clinics

Other Early Deployments

Ambient Documentation Systems

Generative AI for drafting notes and summarizing patient visits (silent mode evaluation)

Cohere Health & Insurance Prior Authorization

Generative AI assisting in insurance prior authorization with strict policy controls

Hippocratic AI

Start-up building safety-focused LLMs for healthcare with ongoing pilot programs

Autonomous AI for Diabetic Retinopathy Screening

FDA Authorization

Received FDA authorization and Medicare reimbursement

Cluster-Randomized Trial

Evaluated across 105 clinic days

Results

40% increase in completed specialist-level eye exams per hour compared to usual care

Demonstrates that autonomous AI can increase throughput while maintaining quality

Key Lessons from Real-World Deployments

1

Local adaptation and integration with existing guidelines builds clinician trust

2

Silent mode evaluation (background operation) is valuable for initial safety assessment

3

Regulatory approval pathways exist but require robust evidence

4

Productivity gains are measurable but must not compromise quality

5 Policy Themes and AI Modalities

Key Regulatory Themes

Risk Management

ISO 14971 and FDA PCCP provide structured processes for hazard identification, risk control, and lifecycle monitoring

Ethics & Human Rights

WHO's guidelines emphasize autonomy, human welfare, transparency, inclusiveness and sustainability

Transparency

HIPAA audit controls, EU AI Act and WHO demand comprehensive logs, impact assessments and traceability

Continuous Monitoring

GMLP, NIST AI RMF and clinical frameworks converge on ongoing performance assessment and drift detection

Stakeholder Engagement

Guidelines call for multi-disciplinary teams, diverse input and human-AI team assessments

Global Coordination

WHO GI-AI4H seeks to harmonize standards across jurisdictions with emphasis on LMIC engagement

Alignment with CAPP

Authorization & Authentication

HIPAA and EU AI Act demand strict access controls - CAPP's OAuth-based design enforces these

Consent-Aware Minimization

WHO calls for transparency in data use - CAPP integrates with FHIR Consent resources

Audit & Documentation

Regulations necessitate comprehensive trails - CAPP uses FHIR AuditEvent

CAPP Operationalizes Policy Requirements

1

Externalizes policy decisions from model weights

2

Aligns with international regulations (HIPAA, EU AI Act, WHO)

3

Facilitates compliance with diverse ethical frameworks

4

Provides transparent control plane for policy enforcement
