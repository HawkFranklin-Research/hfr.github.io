1. Export Law & Regulatory Context

2025 Framework

Export Policy (DGFT)

Regulated via DGFT’s ITC(HS) Schedule-II. In late 2024/2025, policy harmonized to 8-digit ITC(HS) codes.

  • Harmonization: New code alignment with Finance Act 2024 tariffs.
  • Staples Impact: High volatility for Onion (MEP) & Rice (Duties).
  • Fruit Status: Generally "Free" to export, but subject to stringent compliance conditions.

The Real Barrier: SPS & Phytosanitary Protocols

Requirement Layer Details Key Authorities
Orchard/Packhouse Mandatory registration (Annually). Validated for pests/residue. APEDA NPPO
Treatment (VHT/HWT) Vapor Heat / Hot Water Treatment required for Japan, Korea, AU, NZ. NPPO Certified Facilities
Irradiation Mandatory for USA exports. Specific authorized facilities only. USDA-APHIS / NPC
Locs: Vashi, Nashik, B'lore, A'bad

Strategic Insight

For Mangoes, the "Law" allows export, but Market Access is dictacted by technical protocols. USA requires Irradiation; Japan requires VHT. Without access to a registered facility, exports are impossible regardless of DGFT policy.

Gateway Type Function in Mango Chain

Pipavav / Kandla Sea Supporting ecosystem. Host Empty Container Depots (ECDs) and reefer repair services.

5. The Supply Chain Flow

Orchard to Fork

1. Orchard

APEDA Registration required. GAP Compliance. Harvest.

2. Packhouse

Sorting, De-sapping, Washing, Grading, Packing.

3. Treatment

Critical Step. Irradiation (USA) OR VHT (Japan/NZ). NPPO inspection.

4. Logistics

Pre-cooling. Stuffing into Reefers or Air ULDs.

5. Export

Customs, Phyto Cert, COO. Dispatch via Port/Airport.

Key Logistics Units

Sea Freight

20’ / 40’ Reefer Containers. Require active power ("reefer plugs") at port and on vessel to maintain temperature.

Air Freight

ULDs / Insulated Pallets. "Fast lanes" for shelf-life sensitive supply chains.
